AI for Robotics: Asteroids

In this project I used localization techniques to determine the position of my ship and the asteroids in space. The measurment of the distance of the ship from asteroid always contained Gaussian noise. The goal was to then navigate the ship to a safe location without hitting any of the asteroids in the field by determining the next move of the ship at each time step. Approx 5% of the asteroids had a returning tendency from their path. This was implemented in Python using turtle graphics as part of AI for Robotics course by Prof. Jay Summet at GaTech.

AI for Robotics: Mars Glider

In this project, our glider is ejected from a rocket near (0,0) mark but randomly at +/- 250 m. The goal is to localize the glider and glide it safely to landing position before the time runs out. The glider moved at 5m/sec and falls at 1m/sec. We used readings from the barometric sensor to determine the exact location of the glider and land it succesfully on Mars. This was implemented in Python using turtle graphics as part of AI for Robotics course by Prof. Jay Summet at GaTech.
