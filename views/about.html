Principles I Live By

Community Over Competition: Surround yourself with talented, passionate people. The best engineers I know didn't learn in isolation—they learned by collaborating, pair programming, and building alongside others who challenged them to grow.


Teaching Amplifies Learning: The fastest way to truly master a concept is to teach it to someone else. When you explain your code, your architecture decisions, or debug someone else's problem, you deepen your own understanding exponentially.


Read Code, Don't Just Write It: Want to become a better engineer? Read open source code. Study how Django handles middleware, how CPython implements generators, how successful projects structure their architecture. Reading quality code is like learning from the best mentors in the world.


Curiosity is Your Superpower: No bootcamp, course, or mentor can teach you more than your own genuine curiosity. Ask "why" relentlessly. Dig into the source when something doesn't make sense. The most valuable skill isn't knowing the answer—it's knowing how to find it.


Build to Learn, Not Just to Ship: Every project is an opportunity to level up. Don't just copy-paste solutions—understand them. Experiment with different approaches. Break things in staging. The lessons from failures often teach more than successes.



Whether you're looking to architect scalable systems, contribute to open source, or just want to talk tech—let's connect. I believe the best solutions come from collaboration, curiosity, and a relentless commitment to quality.
